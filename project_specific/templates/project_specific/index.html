Hangry

Simplified Food Ordering

What is Hangry?

Hangry is a web app built with Django that solves the problem of ordering lunches in corporations. It stores information about vendors and food, allowing employees to quickly order food

Problem Addressed

For some companies in Asia, employees reguarly orders take-outs for lunch. There is typically a person in charge of ordering food for everyone on their team. This was a rather manual task of asking what each person wants, and keeping track of how much they owe, the total order amount, special notes, etc. Hangry stores information about vendors, food, employees, transactions, and keeps track of all these for you.

The executor (person in charge of ordering food) will get a clear summary of what are the total orders, how much do each employee owe, how much to pay the vendors, the meals breakdown. All orders can be queried and downloaded for record keeping. In addition, employees can keep track of their balance and spending, download their past transactions, and order food online.

How it works

There are three levels of users

  • Superuser
  • Executor
  • Employee
Superuser

As the name implies, superuser can do everything. Some of the common actions include: adding new meals and vendors to the database, creating teams, assigning an executor to the team, changing timezone of organization, manage all users, etc.

Executor

Executor is an employee in charge of collecting the orders and ordering the food. In addition to being an employee, they get a summary of all orders from the team, vendor information, and makes sure all orders are carried out. They also have the power to add/remove people to the team, add balance to an empoyee, download all order histories of the team,

Employee

Employee can order food, see their active orders (food not ordered yet), see their past balance history, download their transaction histories. Employee needs to be a part of team to have these features. In order to be a part of team, executor of the group have to add them in.

Orders of Operation

  1. Sign up for an account using your email
  2. Ask your team's executor to add you into the team
    • If you are an executor, ask the superuser to create a team for you, assign you to the team's executor, and assign your team to your team (the one you are in charge of).
